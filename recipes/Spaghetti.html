Description:

Spaghetti Bolognese is a globally recognised dish orginating in Bologna, Italy. It is consist of long, thin spaghetti with slow cooked hearty meat and tomato sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 sticks celery, finely chopped
  • 500g lean minced beef(ground beef)
  • 2tbsp tomato puree(tomato paste)
  • 125ml dry red wine(optional, use beef stock if preferred)
  • 2 x 400g tins chopped tomatoes
  • 400ml beef stock(made from a stock cube)
  • 1tsp dried oregano or mixed italian herbs
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 tsp Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 400g dried spaghetti
  • Freshly grated parmesan cheese(as much as you want just not to much)
  • 1tsp fresh basil or pasley

Steps:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-based saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Cook gently for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened but not browned
  2. Add the Garlic & Beef: Add the crushed or finely chopped garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the minced beef and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, breaking up any clumps with a wooden spoon, until evenly browned.
  3. Tomato & Wine Base: Stir in the tomato purée and cook for 1 to 2 minutes. Pour in the red wine (if using) and let it bubble for a couple of minutes to reduce slightly. Stir in the tinned chopped tomatoes, beef stock, dried oregano, and bay leaves. Add a generous pinch of salt and pepper
  4. Simmer the Sauce: Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover loosely with a lid and let it cook for about 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally, to develop a rich, deep flavor. If the sauce becomes too thick, add a splash of water.
  5. Cook the Pasta: About 10 minutes before the sauce is ready, bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the packet instructions until it is al dente (tender but with a slight bite). Drain well.
  6. Combine and Serve: Remove the bay leaves from the bolognese sauce and taste for seasoning. You can either mix the drained spaghetti directly into the sauce in the pan, or divide the spaghetti into bowls and spoon the sauce generously over the top. Top with freshly grated Parmesan and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.
